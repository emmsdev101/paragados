Do's and Don'ts of Writing Reports

Writing the Strengths

Strengths hilght the areas or situations where program excels.
Strengths identify areas or situations which reflect Best Practices.
Strengths identify areas or situations which serve as a Model.

Examples:

  1. The DevCom program has been identified as Center of Excellence by the CHED.
  2. library hours are extended up to 9:00 p.m. on Sundays and Saturdays.
  3. Poor but devserving students are accomodated in the Univeristy Dormitory for free in exchange of a four-hour week service rendered as dormitory assistants....

Writing Areas Needing Improvement (ANI)

Areas Needing Improvement (ANI) identifies a specific problem.
Areas Needing Improvement (ANI) identifies a specific problem.

Writing the ANI

Examples:

  1. There is no Extention Plan which can guide the faculty members in implementing extension program/projects/actives
  2. The absense of a covered walk makes it difficult for subjects to tansfer from one building to another when it rains.

Writing Recommentations

  • Recommendations are either mandatory or for enhancement.
  • Mandatory recommendations are for compliance before the award of Levels 3 and 4; while recommendations for enhancement are for compiance after the award of Levels 1, 2, 3, and 4.
  • Recommendations for enhancement should be written in a suggestive, and NOT in a prescriptive manner.
  • Recommendations are premised by the ANI. There should be a one-toone correspondence between the ANI and the recommendations (especially for Levels 1 and 2).
Example 1: Observation (ANI)

There is no Extention Plan, which can guide the faculty members in implementing and monitoring extension programs, projects, and activities (PPAs).

Recommendation
FAculty members are best guided by an Extension Plan in implementing and monitoring extension programs, projects, and activities.

Example 2: Observation (ANI)

Covered walk is absent in the school premisses. The absence makes it difficult to transfer from one building to another when it rains.

Recommendation
The Administration might consider the construction of covered walks in between buildings on campus.

Example 3: Observation (ANI)

Research outputs of the faculty are not published in peer-reviewed journals, which are indexed by Scopus, ISI, Clarivate Analytics, Asian Citiation, and other reputable indexing bodies..

Recommendation
To boost research productivity, it would be desirable if the University encourages the faculty researchesr to publish their research outputs in peer-reviewed journals, which are indexed by Scopus or other reputable indexing bodies. Publication support and incentives would be very helpful in achieving research productivity.

Writing the Final Report

  • Findings as bases for recommendations should be document-based and validated through interviews, and other nesessary procedures/measures.
  • The Narrative Profile does not cover all claims. As much as possible we have to use triangulation for a verified, valid and reliable report.
  • The Narrative Profile may exaggerate or understate claims. Some claims are well supported, in some cases, you need to look for it.
  • The absence of evidence is not necesarrily the evidence of absence.
  • Never presume anything. Look for hard pieces of evidence.
  • Do not be taken in by glossy brochures. Marketing and promotions materials are different form reality.
  • Findings should be congruent with the ratings.
  • Approach the writing in a factual, analytical, and reflective manner to achieve a reasonable feedback report.
  • Refrain from giving opinions, perceptions, prescriptions, and motherhood statements. Substantiate very statement made.
  • Demonstrate objective professional judgment, free from bias or preconceived notions, thoroughly validated, and verified.
  • Use clear, simple, and easily comprehensible language.
  • Reflect rudiments of grammar, syntax, and logic
  • Avoid jargons (special term or expression of a particular group or field).
  • Ideas should not conflict with each other.
  • State facts straight to the point.
  • Avoid verbosity or verbiage (the use of too many words to express an idea).
  • No beating around the bush.
  • Avoid derogatory remkarks or disparaging words.
  • Avoid unreasonable recommendations.
  • In case the score initially given by the area lead accreditor is changed during the team deliberation, the area lead accreditor should ensure that the comments and suggestions of the other accreditors are incorporated in the feedback report to reflect the new score.
  • Proff reading and editing are necessary.
Common errors in writing the final report
Writing sweeping generalizations.
Lifting statements verbatim from the benchmark statements making it as strengths.
Lacking of congruence between observations (ANI) and recommendations.
Overuse of words: wanting, limited, revisit.
Repetitions use of phrases: if funds, warrant, it is recommendd.
Erroneous subject-verb agreement..
Inappropriate or wrong use or words.
Misspelled words: accommodate, recommend, assimilate, occasion, satellite, liaison, etc.
Errors in parallelism.
Recommendations are stated only in words or phrases.
Abridged report.
Use of many superlatives to acknowledge people, especially school officials.
Copy-paste reporting.

Preparing the Team Leader's Report

Fill out the forms properly provided for the purpose. Do not leave any item unanswered. Put NA for items that are not applicable.
Write in short, simple, declarative sentences. KISS - Keep it short and simple!

Actual Written Reports

Area III - Parameter C

Assessment of Academic Performance

General Observations:

  1. The lay out of the soft copies submitted for evaluation is neat and aesthetically present although given the contexts, it lacks supporting documents to substantiate some enumerated claims.
  2. Some scanned supporting forms and documents are not authenticated.

Strengths

  1. There is a system of evaluating student performance using a combination of a variety of evaluation measures and strategies duly implemented as shown in the documents submitted.
  2. The students’ academic performance is commendable as shown in the considerable number of graduates with honors and even high honors.

Areas Needing Improvement

  1. Samples of forms and documents submitted are limited. Only one or two samples of exams and TOS are presented.
  2. Some scanned supporting forms and documents are not authenticated.
  3. Retention rate of students is not presented.
Recommendations
  1. It is recommended that the College may consider substantiating their claims through submitting a number of supporting documents to give adequate bases for validation and rating.
  2. The College my consider authenticating documents before submission to establish their credibility.
  3. The College may consider presenting the retention rate of the students as it is one of the major indicators of its excellent output and effectivity.
