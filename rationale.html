The rationale for Conducting Accreditation

Importance of accreditation in ensuring quality education

Accreditation aims for and promotes quality education. It is very important if not indispensable to the whole educational system particularly in ensuring that program offerings are at par with standards set by authorities and accrediting bodies. When program offerings and institutions are accredited, the ones who most benefit are the major clients of the educational institutions who are the students.

Consequently, parents become confident that their children are in good hands and that they get the worth of their money. Even if we say that public education in the Philippines is free across all levels, accreditation is a means to determine that the taxpayersâ€™ money is well spent. After all, quality education is the thrust of all stakeholders of an educational institution.

That is why, even in accreditation processes, there is an involvement or presence of students, alumni, community and local government officials and whose views and ideas become sources of information if not strong bases for triangulation of claims the institution or the program being accredited are establishing.

Explanation of how accreditation enhances program design and evaluation

Accreditation enhances program design and evaluation since it is the very core of this process. It establishes the credibility of offering programs by higher education institutions. The meticulous process of accreditation spells out the very essence of evaluation to ensure its ultimate end which is quality education.

Accreditation ensures also that as degree offering advances in terms of number of years which it is being offered, the quality of its systems and inputs, its implementation as well as its outputs also advances accordingly.

It also promotes quality of program offerings by setting benchmarks amoung institutions with lower-level accreditations with those who have already advanced in the field of accreditation.

Personal insights

Personally, I always believe that accreditation is a potent tool to ensure quality and relevant education. My a little more than two decades of accreditation experiences in the institution where I render service as a faculty member has seen tremendous improvements and developments that are in place now because of accreditations that have been conducted so far.

While meeting the standards of the level a program in trying to get and eventually pass is definitely tedious and demanding physically, emotionally and mentally; nevertheless, the positive result when the level applied for is granted, is fulfilling and gratifying.

All stakeholders become happy afterwards at the thought that the institution that they all support and form part of has indeed reasons for existing and for continuing to offer degree programs found to be of quality and relevant by the accrediting body.
