The Accreditation Proccess: A brief overview and its significance

Accreditation is a form of evaluation. It is a process of looking into the strengths, and weaknesses as well as areas which are needing improvement of programs and institutions in certain parameters determined or established by the accrediting body, normally a third party or an external party. It is a means of ensuring that programs and administration of these programs are within acceptable standards within the level they are being accredited. The ultimate end of accreditation is quality assurance.

Accreditation is important for a number of reasons. It provides assurance to the public that an organization program or institution has met established standards of quality, and that it has undergone a rigorous evaluation process by an independent accrediting agency. Accreditation can also help organizations to improve their operations and outcomes by providing them with feedback and guidance on areas where they need to improve.

For educational institutions, accreditation is particularly important because it can impact a student’s ability to transfer credits to other institutions or to obtain professional licensure. Many employers also look for job candidates who have graduated from accredited institutions as it is seen as a mark of quality and rigor.

In the Philippines, state and even local colleges and universities are submitting their curricular programs and course offering to AACCUP for accreditation. Normally, AACCUP evaluates 10 areas (10 Standards/ Key Result Areas) in all programs it evaluates. These areas are :

  • Area I : Vision, Mission, Goals, and Objectives
  • Area II : Faculty
  • Area III : Curriculum and Instruction
  • Area IV: Support to Students
  • Area V : Research
  • Area VI: Extension and Community Involvement Area
  • VII: Library Area
  • VIII: Physical Plant and Facilities
  • Area IX: Laboratories
  • Area X : Administration

A certain degree program being submitted for accreditation has to obtain the desired over-all mean of these areas to be granted or awarded the level of accreditation it is applying for. As the level advances, so is the over-all mean needed to obtain the accreditation. That is to say, Level 1 needs a lower over-all mean rating compared to Level 2, and so on.

Every area has parameters with benchmarks/standards that need to be satisfied. For example, Area 3 – Curriculum and Instruction, has six parameters and each parameter has indicators which the accreditor concerned has to rate (Item Rating) from NA (Not Applicable) to 5 (Excellent) and solve for the System – Implementation-Outcome (SIOM) Mean and eventually, for the Parameter Mean (PM) based on physical and triangulated evidences and documents presented.

To illustrate, Area 3 Parameter A has 3 System Inputs and Processes. System 1 has 3 benchmarks or indicators. So, each indicator should be rated by the accreditor with either NA, 0, 1, 2, 3, 4 or 5 as the case may be and placed at the end of each indicator in the accreditation instrument. Then the means of the 3 indicators should be reflected in the SIOM column in the same instrument. When all the indicators – each in the System Inputs and Processes, Implementation and Outcome/s – have been rated, the Parameter Mean (M) shall now be computed by adding the 3 means and dividing the sum by three. The result will be the rating of the program in Parameter A of Area 3.

VISSION OF AACCUP

The long-range vision of AACCUP is to develop among SUCs a “culture of quality” such that quality assurance is then rested inside the school system, that SUCs shall maintain a certified roster of quality programs and processes.

MISSION OF AACCUP

To make the attainment of quality in education an integral part of the higher education systems more particularly among chartered state colleges and universities in the Philippines, through a sustained program of internal and external assessment. Under its charter, one of the functions, if not the main purpose of AACCUP, is "to develop a mechanism of, and conduct the evaluation of programs and institutions."

GOALS OF AACCUP

The AACCUP aims to :

  • Develop a mechanism for, and conduct the evaluation of programs and institutions for accreditation;
  • Develop a mechanism for, and conduct the evaluation of programs and institutions for accreditation;
  • establish policies and develop standards and instruments for evaluating programs/institutions;
  • generate public understanding of, earn acceptance and adapt the professional practice of accreditation to the Philippine setting; and
  • perform other activities that may directly or indirectly lead to the attainment of the purposes of accreditation.
