the actual Accreditation Process

The whole activity started with planning where everyone of us – students- as well as our course facilitator was involved and actively participated.

Two or three physical class sessions were devoted to planning. Originally, the class was divided into three groups: one to visit and evaluate a private junior high school in Bacolod City, Negros Occidental, one in University of Antique and one in the main campus of ISUFST. However, due to a reason beyond anyone’s control, the class settled to visit the last two state universities.

Specific students were identified who should go with the ISUFST group and who should go with the University of Antique. Both were to conduct a mock accreditation for the institutions BSED (English and Math) degree offerings, specifically, in Area III only using the accreditation instrument of AACCUP.

A session was spent online in the briefing by the course Professor, Dr. Sybel Joy F. Labis on the concept of AACCUP accreditation and on how to use its instruments.

Contingencies anent the activity like food and snacks, class uniform, transportation, itinerary, etc. were also included in the planning.

I belonged to the group assigned to visit ISUFST Main Campus in Tiwi, Barotac Nuevo, Iloilo on May 12, 2023.

Below was the itinerary for our group:

  • The group arrived at the area on time. Upon arrival, Dr. Labis and the rest made a courtesy call with the University President, Dr. Nordy Siason who was around that time.
  • Starting at 2:00 o’ clock PM and the next two hours we conducted the mock accreditation. There were two groups: one for the BSED major in English curriculum and the another for the BSED major in Math curriculum. I belonged to the former group.
  • At around 3:30 o’ clock PM, the class started sharing each group’s outputs with Dr. Labis facilitating. The discussion was enriching and much have been learned from the insights of one another and from the wisdom of the course professor. When the sharing had been through, the group started to pick up, did a short tour of the campus and took some groufies and pictures before parted ways to go each home.

Explanation of the roles and responsibilities of the accreditation team members

The Accreditation Team Organization :

  1. The Over-all Coordinator. This is the case when there are 3-4 programs being accredited in one institution. Under the Over-all Coordinator are the :
    • Team Leaders and the
    • Lead Accreditors/Members
  2. The Over-all Coordinator (OC) : Assumes Over-all Leadership
    • Coordinates the activities of the team
    • Presidess over joint meetings
    • Serve as liason working with his/her counterpart in the host institution
    • Gives orientation on accreditation process
    • Acts as Spokeperson during the exit conference
    • Serves as back up accreditor in the different areas.
  3. The Team Leader
    • Assumes leadership of the survey team in a program under review
    • Collaborates with the OC in supervising the activities of the team
    • Calls and presides over team meetings
    • Collects the filled up accreditation instrument at the end of the online visit
    • Assists the members of the team
    • Consolidates and rewrites the final team report and submits it to OC
  4. The Lead Accreditors/Members
    • Work on their area of assignment and cooperates in making a well prepared report
    • Be familiar with contents of the self-survey instrument, compliance report, PPP and survey instruments
    • Make evaluation based on the survey instrument and rating system
    • Be good team player
    • Prepare a well-analyzed and written summary of findings and recommendations
    • Always act professionally

Insights and challenges encountered during the accreditation process

Accreditation as an activity or process is not new to me, as I mentioned above. I have helped during the accreditations of several programs in the university campus I am serving. So practically, the activity we conducted at ISUFST Main Campus was generally smooth sailing for me personally.

However, it is inevitable that there are some minor challenges that I encountered during the activity day. Firstly, the distance from my place. The site we visited was quite far from our place and since I have no private car or service vehicle, I had to commute and had to ask a classmate who has a car to wait for me in Zarraga, Iloilo so I can hitch ride with him along with our professor and two of my classmates. Since travelling by bus is not predictable, I came late in our rendezvous. I was ashamed to let them wait for me. I hoped they did not wait for me for a long time.

Secondly, travelling for me is always a challenge since I take maintenance medications for my hypertension and diabetes. We arrived at the site on time but I had to eat my lunch first in their campus cafeteria for me to take my meds. As a consequence, I started my tasks quite a little late than the rest of the group.

Thirdly, the documents given to us for evaluation were scanned copies the University submitted to AACCUP last year when they applied for Level 4 status of their BSED program with all the field of specializations under it. The fact that the AACCUP itself has closed the system, it was a real challenge for us to verify the claims especially that most of the claims or documents were supplemented by supporting documents that were hyperlinked but since the whole sets of documents were in PDF form, there was no way for us to click and open those links. Triangulation then was a definite challenge.

Finally, although we only looked into Area III - Curriculum and Instruction for only one specialization (I was assigned to BSED - English group), the time given to us to check the documents against the benchmarks in the six parameters of Area III was too short for us to have done much. It would have been more enriching if time would not have been limited. If we were to count, our travel time to and from that place was just equal or even longer that the time we spent for the actual doing of the thing.

Lessons learned and personal growth from conducting the Accreditation

Generally, the activity was very enriching, fruitful and productyive. There were many things I personally learned from the whole process starting from the planning stage until the culmination of the accreditation day. For one, accreditation is a teamwork. Cooperation and participation of everyone in the Team are significant for the attainment of the objectives of the activity.

Another is that, studying about the things to be done and how to do them is also very important. There is no better substitute than to go to the battle armed and ready.

Next, evaluation is a process of being reasonable, just and fair. Making judgments should never rely on what are being presented per se. Triangulation is a must. If an accreditor thinks that the documents presented are lacking, he/she should find all means to verify the claims. He/she should always check and go over his /her ratings given and compromise or adjust if needed.

Finally, the activity refreshed and enhanced the training I attended with ACCUP on Outcomes-based Accreditation. Though I have not been called by AACCUP to serve as accreditor so far, yet I completed a short term training course on it. The activity is a welcoming prelude to the Level 4 accreditation that we are preparing for come August or September.
